# 30-60-90 Plan

## Guiding Rule

Diagnose first. Validate second. Act with evidence third.

Platform organizations pay a high price when a new PM installs rituals before understanding why the current shape exists. The entry plan at Meridian followed three phases: diagnose, validate, act.

## Before Day 1

### Inputs

- active roadmap and milestones
- current GitHub Projects structure
- recent incident and postmortem history
- current observability stack, SLOs, alerting coverage, and upgrade metrics
- release calendar across Kubernetes and self-hosted surfaces
- recurring blocker and dependency list

### Alignment to establish

- what success means in 6 months
- which decisions belong to the PM versus leadership
- where current delivery feels weakest
- where leadership has lowest confidence today

### Output

A mandate note: scope, success criteria, decision rights, and open questions.

## Days 1-30: Diagnose

| Goal | Actions | Outputs |
|---|---|---|
| Understand how work really flows and where the highest-cost seams are | Interviewed platform engineering, leadership, support, solution engineering, customer success, and security; inventoried active work, blockers, interrupt load, and observability gaps; mapped the release path across Kubernetes promotions, Helm charts, Docker Compose, and appliance images; reviewed recent incidents, SLA pain, and upgrade failures; drafted the first scorecard | Assessment memo, risk map, dependency seam map, draft scorecard, shared vocabulary for themes and statuses |

## Days 31-60: Validate And Install The Rhythm

| Goal | Actions | Outputs |
|---|---|---|
| Pressure-test findings, install the minimum viable operating model, and remove obvious coordination waste | Reviewed findings with platform leadership; launched weekly delivery triage, dependency sync, and release review; cleaned up active work tracking; established a decision log; defined 3-5 roadmap themes; picked one flagship initiative | Operating model v1 in use, roadmap themes validated, scorecard v1 live, flagship initiative selected and aligned |

## Days 61-90: Act With Evidence

| Goal | Actions | Outputs |
|---|---|---|
| Turn visibility into changed behavior and prove the PM function increases leverage | Published the first roadmap and risk view; launched the flagship initiative; closed 2-3 chronic blockers; installed the incident-to-roadmap loop; began monthly health reviews; tightened ETA language so confidence is explicit | One shared view of roadmap and delivery health, flagship initiative underway, clearer dependency management, monthly health review rhythm |

## Day 90 Success Signals

| Area | Signal |
|---|---|
| Delivery visibility | Leadership sees what is on track, blocked, and risky without a manual chase |
| Team leverage | Engineers wait less on context, decisions, or partner follow-through |
| Roadmap quality | Work is grouped into themes and outcomes, not isolated technical tasks |
| Operational learning | Incidents and upgrade pain visibly change backlog and sequencing |
| Trust | Platform leadership sees the PM as a force multiplier, not an extra layer |
