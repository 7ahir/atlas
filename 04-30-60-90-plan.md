# 30-60-90 Plan

## Guiding Rule

Diagnose first. Validate second. Act with evidence third.

Platform organizations pay dearly when a new PM installs rituals before understanding why the current shape exists.

## Before Day 1

### Inputs I want

- active roadmap and milestones
- current GitHub Projects structure
- recent incident and postmortem history
- existing platform KPIs, SLOs, and upgrade metrics
- release calendar across cloud and self-hosted surfaces
- recurring blocker and dependency list

### Alignment I want

- what success means in 6 months
- which decisions belong to the PM versus leadership
- where current delivery feels weakest
- where leadership has lowest confidence today

### Output

A mandate note: scope, success criteria, decision rights, and open questions.

## Days 1-30: Diagnose

| Goal | Actions | Outputs |
|---|---|---|
| Understand how work really flows and where the highest-cost seams are | Interview platform engineering, leadership, support, solution engineering, and customer-facing partners; inventory active work, blockers, and interrupt load; map the release path; review recent incidents and upgrade failures; draft the first scorecard | Assessment memo, risk map, dependency seam map, draft scorecard, shared vocabulary for themes and statuses |

## Days 31-60: Validate And Install The Rhythm

| Goal | Actions | Outputs |
|---|---|---|
| Pressure-test findings, install the minimum viable operating model, and remove obvious coordination waste | Review findings with platform leadership; launch weekly delivery triage, dependency sync, and release review; clean up active work tracking; establish a decision log; define 3-5 roadmap themes; pick one flagship initiative | Operating model v1 in use, roadmap themes validated, scorecard v1 live, flagship initiative selected and aligned |

## Days 61-90: Act With Evidence

| Goal | Actions | Outputs |
|---|---|---|
| Turn visibility into changed behavior and prove the PM function increases leverage | Publish the first roadmap and risk view; launch the flagship initiative; close 2-3 chronic blockers; install the incident-to-roadmap loop; begin monthly health reviews; tighten ETA language so confidence is explicit | One shared view of roadmap and delivery health, flagship initiative underway, clearer dependency management, monthly health review rhythm |

## Day 90 Success Signals

| Area | Signal |
|---|---|
| Delivery visibility | Leadership can see what is on track, blocked, and risky without a manual chase |
| Team leverage | Engineers are waiting less on context, decisions, or partner follow-through |
| Roadmap quality | Work is grouped into themes and outcomes, not isolated technical tasks |
| Operational learning | Incidents and upgrade pain visibly change backlog and sequencing |
| Trust | Platform leadership sees the PM as a force multiplier, not an extra layer |
